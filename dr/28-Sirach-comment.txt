*** chapter 4 ***

18 "In temptation": The meaning is, that before wisdom will choose any for her favourite, she will try them by leading them through contradictions, afflictions, and temptations, the usual noviceship of the children of God.

*** chapter 5 ***


*** chapter 6 ***


*** chapter 7 ***

15 "Repeat not": Make not much babbling by repetition of words: but aim more at fervour of heart.
33 "Thy arms": That is, with all thy power: or else by arms (brachiis) are here signified the right shoulders of the victims, which by the law fell to the priests. See ver. 35.
37 "And restrain not grace from the dead": That is, withhold not from them the benefit of alms, prayers, and sacrifices. Such was the doctrine and practice of the church of God even in the time of the Old Testament. And the same has always been continued from the days of the apostles in the church of the New Testament.

*** chapter 12 ***

*** chapter 14 ***

12 "Covenant of hell": The decree by which all are to go down to the regions of death.

*** chapter 15 ***


*** chapter 16 ***

11 "Six hundred thousand footmen": Viz., the children of Israel, whom he sentenced to die in the wilderness. Num. 14.
31 "shewn forth": Viz., the glory and power of God upon the earth.

*** chapter 17 ***

11 "Their eye saw": Viz., when he gave the law on mount Sinai.
22 "Offend less": Minue offendicula. That is, remove sins and the occasions of sins.
25 "Go to the side": Fly from the side of Satan and sin, and join with the holy ones, that follow God and godliness.

*** chapter 18 ***

6 "Then shall he begin": God is so great and incomprehensible, that when man has done all that he can to find out his greatness and boundless perfections, he is still to begin: for what he has found out, is but a mere nothing in comparison with his infinity.

*** chapter 19 ***


*** chapter 20 ***


*** chapter 21 ***

30 "While the ungodly": He condemneth and curseth himself: inasmuch as by sin he takes part with the devil, and is, as it were, his member and subject.

*** chapter 22 ***

10 "For the fool": In the language of the Holy Ghost, he is styled a fool, that turns away from God to follow vanity and sin. And what is said by the wise man against fools is meant of such fools as these.

*** chapter 23 ***

1 "By them": Viz., the tongue and the lips, mentioned in the last verse of the foregoing chapter.
2 "That they spare me not in their ignorances": That is, that the scourges and discipline of wisdom may restrain the ignorances, that is, the slips and offences which are usually committed by the tongue and the lips.

*** chapter 24 ***

34 "A most mighty king": Viz., Christ, who by his gospel, like an overflowing river, has enriched the earth with heavenly wisdom.
38 "Who first hath perfect knowledge of her": Christ was the first that had perfect knowledge of heavenly wisdom.

*** chapter 25 ***


*** chapter 26 ***

28 "From negligence": That is, from the neglect of the service of God: because the eager pursuit of the mammon of this world, is apt to make men of that calling forget the great duties of loving God above all things, and their neighbours as themselves.-- Ibid.
28 "A huckster": Or, a retailer of wine. Men of that profession are both greatly exposed to danger of sin themselves, and are too often accessary to the sins of others.

*** chapter 27 ***

23 "And of a curse there is reconciliation": That is, it is easier to obtain a reconciliation after a curse, than after disclosing a secret.

*** chapter 28 ***

7 "In his commandments": Supply the sentence out of the Greek thus: Remember corruption and death, and abide in the commandments.

*** chapter 29 ***

1 "And he that is stronger in hand": That is, he that is hearty and bountiful in lending to his neighbour in his necessity.

*** chapter 32 ***

27 "In faith": That is, follow sincerely thy soul in her faith and conscience.

*** chapter 33 ***

21 "Change thee": That is, so as to have this power over thee.
23 "The pre-eminence": That is, be master in thy own house, and part not with thy authority.

*** chapter 36 ***

23 "A woman will receive every man": That is, any man that her parents propose to her to marry, though she does not like him, but marries in obedience to her parents, who make the choice for her.

*** chapter 37 ***


*** chapter 38 ***

25 "A scribe": That is, a doctor of the law, or, a learned man.

*** chapter 39 ***

17 "Ye divine offspring": He speaks to the children of Israel, the people of God: whom he exhorts to bud forth and flourish with virtue.

*** chapter 40 ***


*** chapter 41 ***

19 "Have a shame": That is to say, be ashamed of doing any of these things, which I am now going to mention; for though sometimes shamefacedness is not to be indulged: yet it is often good and necessary: as in the following cases.

*** chapter 42 ***

14 "Better is the iniquity": That is, there is, commonly speaking, less danger to be apprehended to the soul from the churlishness, or injuries we receive from men, than from the flattering favours and familiarity of women.

*** chapter 49 ***

18 "They prophesied": That is, by their being carried out of Egypt they verified the prophetic prediction of Joseph. Gen. 50.

*** chapter 50 ***

11 "Clothed with the perfection of power": That is, with all the vestments denoting his dignity and authority.
27 "Abhorreth": Viz., with a holy indignation, as enemies of God and persecutors of his people. Such were then the Edomites who abode in mount Seir, the Philistines, and the Samaritans who dwelt in Sichem, and had their schismatical temple in that neighbourhood.
